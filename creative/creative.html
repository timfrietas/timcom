Ocean Waves

Ocean Waves is the first of a series of Processing sketches attempting to recreate Amish quilt patterns.

I've always felt Amish quilts would make very interesting Processing sketches--they are full of geometry and color, and they've been trapped as static patterns this whole time just begging to be animated. There's certainly a lot more I'd like to do with animation and color regarding this particular pattern, but it proved interesting just to get it to tile correctly in the first place!

The Ocean Waves pattern is really only twelve repeating triangles, which are each flipped and/or rotated to form a complete square tile. These are chained together in groups of four to make a row, then each row is in turn flipped and alternated to create the final pattern.

The only animation you see here is each individual triangle rotating in place on its center. Every 90 degrees this produces an interesting pattern as the triangles "align"; the pattern begins on the original. I have some fun variations in mind which I will almost certainly revisit later after I complete some of the other patterns that I'd like to try first.

Scatterwaves

Scatterwaves was literally modeled after watching the Silly Walks sketch by Monty Python and I made the connection between silly pun (sketch, har har) and trying to create something seemingly asymmetrical but that loops gracefully.

If you look at the code you'll obviously recognize I started with a beesaandbombs sketch, as he does some nice tricks to ensure he gets a seamless loop from everything.
